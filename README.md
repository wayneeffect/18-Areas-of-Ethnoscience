# What's New in 18 Areas of Ethnoscience (2026)

**Curated monthly snapshots of cutting-edge developments** at the intersection of traditional knowledge, cultural practices, and modern science.

This repository archives an ongoing conversation exploring rapid advancements across ethnobiology, ethnomedicine, and related interdisciplinary fields as of mid-2026.

## Overview

In a time of accelerating scientific progress and renewed appreciation for Indigenous and traditional knowledge systems, this series examines **what's new** in 18 key domains of ethnoscience and its neighboring disciplines.

From laboratory breakthroughs to field ethnographies, these updates highlight how ancient wisdom and contemporary research are converging to address climate resilience, health innovation, conservation, and sustainability.

## Topics Covered

### Core Ethnoscience & Ethnobiology
- **Ethnobiology** – Biocultural diversity, climate resilience, and decolonial approaches
- **Ethnobotany** – Traditional plant knowledge, quantitative methods, and conservation
- **Ethnozoology** – Human-animal relationships, traditional medicine, and wildlife co-management
- **Ethnomicrobiology** – Human-microbe interactions, fermentation, and microbial heritage
- **Ethnomedicine** – Traditional healing systems and their modern relevance
- **Ethnoforestry** – Traditional forest management, Indigenous co-stewardship, and restoration
- **Ethnopharmacology** – Scientific validation of traditional medicines and drug discovery
- **Pharmacognosy** – Natural products research, standardization, and quality control

### Related Scientific & Medical Fields
- **Cultural Ethnography** & **Medical Anthropology** – Contemporary health cultures, care practices, and biopolitics
- **Integrative Health & Medicine** – Whole-person care and evidence-based complementary approaches
- **Botany & Plant Science** – Evolutionary insights, genetic advances, and climate adaptation
- **Pharmacology & Drug Discovery** – AI-driven development, new modalities, and precision medicine
- **Bioengineering** – Xenotransplantation, regenerative medicine, and synthetic biology
- **Quantum Biology** – Quantum effects in living systems and emerging biological qubits

### Cross-Cutting Domains
- **Bioethics** – Governance of organoids, xenotransplantation, AI in medicine, and planetary health

*(Total: 18 distinct but interconnected areas)*

## Purpose

This series serves as:
- A living knowledge base for researchers, practitioners, and students
- A bridge between traditional ecological knowledge (TEK) and frontier science
- A monthly “state of the field” reference for interdisciplinary thinkers
- Inspiration for new collaborations at the culture–science interface

## Structure

Each update includes:
- Major trends and breakthroughs
- Notable publications and journals
- Upcoming conferences and community events
- Practical resources and deeper-dive suggestions
