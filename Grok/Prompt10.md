**Ethnomedicine**—the study of traditional, indigenous, and culturally specific medical systems and practices—is advancing rapidly in 2026. It bridges cultural anthropology, ethnobotany, ethnopharmacology, and modern biomedical sciences, with a focus on documenting knowledge, validating efficacy, ensuring sustainability, and integrating traditional systems into global health amid climate, biodiversity, and equity challenges.

### Key Trends and Developments

- **Documentation, Bibliometrics, and Regional Studies**: Extensive research continues on regional practices (e.g., Nigerian ethnobotanical uses analyzed for trends from 1964–2024, highlighting knowledge gaps and healthcare contributions). Bibliometric and quantitative approaches are increasingly common.

- **Integration with Modern Science and Technology**: Emphasis on linking traditional knowledge with evidence-based validation, phytochemistry, bioactivity studies, and drug discovery. Digital tools, AI (e.g., generative AI for ethnopharmacology), DNA barcoding, GIS, and computational methods support analysis, preservation, and innovation while addressing standardization and quality.

- **Sustainability, Biocultural Conservation, and Decolonial Perspectives**: Focus on conserving medicinal plants, indigenous knowledge sovereignty, and sustainable use amid environmental pressures. Special issues explore women in research, African frontiers, and globalization of traditional medicine.

- **Applications in Health and Pharmacology**: Studies on bioactive compounds, antimicrobial resistance, herbal formulations, and integrative approaches. Ethnopharmacology plays a key role in bridging tradition with evidence-based healthcare.

### Publications and Journals
- **Journal of Ethnobiology and Ethnomedicine** (Springer): Active with 20+ years of impact; special collections on digital pathways, AI/computational integration, and African ethnobiology.
- **Journal of Ethnopharmacology**: Special issues on quality/standardization of botanicals, high-content screening for Chinese medicine, and reimagining ethnopharmacology.
- **Ethnobotany Research and Applications** (Vol. 34, 2026): Features quantitative methods, spatial availability scores, and reviews.
- Other outlets: *American Journal of Ethnomedicine*, *Frontiers in Pharmacology* (Reviews in Ethnopharmacology 2025), and special issues in *Plants* and related journals.

The field balances urgent documentation of at-risk knowledge with rigorous scientific validation, ethical collaboration (especially with Indigenous communities), and practical applications in integrative health and drug development. Challenges include intellectual property, sustainability, and equitable benefit-sharing.
