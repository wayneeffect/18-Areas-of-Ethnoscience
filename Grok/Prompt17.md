**Ethnopharmacology**—the scientific study of traditional medicines, their biological activities, and their potential for modern drug development—continues to grow rapidly in 2026. It bridges cultural knowledge (from ethnobotany, ethnozoology, and ethnomicrobiology) with rigorous pharmacology, phytochemistry, quality control, and translational research, often incorporating AI and advanced screening technologies.

### Key Trends and Advances
- **AI Integration and Translational Research**: Major conferences and programs highlight AI’s role in accelerating ethnopharmacological data analysis, compound screening, and bridging traditional knowledge with modern healthcare. Examples include predictive modeling for bioactivity and standardization.

- **Quality, Standardization, and Bridging Traditions with Science**: Strong focus on botanical quality control, safety, and evidence-based validation. Special issues address high-content screening for Chinese medicine, honoring leaders in the field, and global health applications.

- **Reverse Pharmacology and Drug Discovery**: Continued emphasis on ethnopharmacology-inspired approaches to speed up discovery, including synergistic formulations, multi-target agents, and validation of traditional remedies for conditions like metabolic diseases, inflammation, cancer, and antimicrobial resistance.

- **Biocultural and Interdisciplinary Work**: Research expands on plants, animals, fungi, and minerals, with growing attention to sustainability, Indigenous rights, and integration into One Health or planetary health frameworks.

### Publications and Journals
- **Journal of Ethnopharmacology** (official journal of the ISE): Active volumes in 2026 with special issues from prior congresses, high-content screening, quality/standardization (e.g., in honor of Prof. Ikhlas A. Khan), and ongoing field studies.
- **Frontiers in Pharmacology**: “Reviews in Ethnopharmacology: 2025” series highlighting recent advances and future directions.
- Regional and society journals from India’s Society for Ethnopharmacology and others.

- **25th International Congress of the International Society for Ethnopharmacology (ISE)**: September 14–17, Foligno, Italy. Theme around “Ethnopharmacology and Transcultural Medicine” — a crossroads of culture and science. First time in Italy.
- **13th International Congress of the Society for Ethnopharmacology (SFEC-ICTRE 2026)**: February 26–28, NIPER Mohali, India. Theme: “Translational Research in Ethnopharmacology — Integrating Traditional Medicine in Modern Healthcare,” with strong AI focus.
- Other events: Multiple international conferences on ethnopharmacology and pharmacognosy throughout the year.

The field maintains momentum in documenting at-risk traditional knowledge while advancing evidence-based applications, regulatory acceptance, and equitable benefit-sharing. It overlaps heavily with pharmacognosy, ethnomedicine, integrative health, and drug discovery.
