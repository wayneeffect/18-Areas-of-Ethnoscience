**Ethnobiology**—the interdisciplinary study of human interactions with biological organisms (plants, animals, microbes, fungi, etc.) across cultures, past and present—is flourishing in 2026. It emphasizes biocultural diversity, Indigenous and local knowledge (ILK), conservation, climate resilience, decolonial approaches, and forward-looking applications in sustainability and health.

### Major Trends and Developments

- **Decolonial, Ethical, and Collaborative Frameworks**: Ongoing critiques of colonial legacies in the field, with calls to prioritize Indigenous sovereignty, intellectual property rights, and equitable partnerships. Papers address challenges like local knowledge in environmental management and rethinking terms like "ethno." Forward-looking ethnobiology integrates anticipatory methods for co-creating futures with communities.

- **Climate Change, Fire Ecology, and Resilience**: Special sections and sessions on fire ecology, climate adaptation, pastoralism, and agrobiodiversity conservation. Research explores diaspora knowledge, migrant practices, and how communities navigate ecological changes.

- **Urban, Diaspora, and Hybrid Knowledges**: Growing attention to urban ethnobiology, migrant-led practices, DIY herbalism, and digital cultures. Studies examine in-situ conservation in changing landscapes and borderlands.

- **Ethics, Governance, and Biocultural Conservation**: Work on cultural keystone places, heritage management, Gitxsan legal orders, and tools for data sharing that uphold Indigenous rights. Degrowth perspectives and policy-relevant contributions are emerging.

- **Intersections with Related Fields**: Strong overlaps with ethnobotany, ethnomedicine, ethnomicrobiology, and integrative health, including ritual plant use, pest management, and wild foods.

### Key Publications (2025–2026)
- **Journal of Ethnobiology**: 2025 Volume 45 (with special sections on fire, climate, pastoralism); 2026 issues including special issues on borderlands (Mexico–US) and more.
- **Journal of Ethnobiology and Ethnomedicine**: Active with recent articles on ritual plants, pest control, coffee disease management, and wild food knowledge.
- **Ethnobiology Letters**: Open-access perspectives and reviews.
- Other: *Ethnobiology and Conservation*, Recife Charter on future pathways, and reflections on European/global ethnobiology.

The field is shifting toward more inclusive, action-oriented, and anticipatory research that supports planetary health, cultural resilience, and justice. It balances deep documentation with applied outcomes in conservation, food systems, and policy.
