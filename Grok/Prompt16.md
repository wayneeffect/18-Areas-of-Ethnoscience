**Ethnozoology**—the study of human-animal relationships through cultural, historical, ecological, and practical lenses (including traditional knowledge, uses in medicine/rituals, hunting, taboos, and conservation)—is advancing within the broader field of **ethnobiology**. As of mid-2026, it emphasizes decolonial approaches, Indigenous co-management, biocultural conservation, and applications to wildlife management amid climate and biodiversity crises.

### Key Trends and Recent Research
- **Traditional Animal-Based Medicine and Knowledge Systems**: Studies document ethnozoological practices, such as animal-derived remedies among Indigenous communities (e.g., a 2025 study on Jhargram, West Bengal, India, exploring wild animal medicines). Similar work appears in the Philippines, Africa, and Latin America, often linking to pharmacognosy and integrative health.

- **Conservation, Co-Management, and Indigenous Knowledge**: Emphasis on integrating local ecological knowledge into wildlife management, protected areas, and restoration. This includes cultural keystone species, human-wildlife conflict resolution, and critiques of colonial legacies in conservation.

- **Emerging Topics**: De-extinction science (e.g., dire wolf discussions with anti-colonial perspectives), urban/wildlife interfaces, scatology (use of animal feces), and ethical dimensions of human-animal entanglements. Horizon scans for conservation in 2026 highlight Indigenous rights and funding mechanisms.

- **Interdisciplinary Links**: Strong overlaps with wildlife biology, ethnobiology, zooarchaeology, and One Health approaches. New species discoveries (over 16,000 annually) and faster documentation rates enrich ethnozoological baselines.

### Publications and Journals
- **Journal of Ethnobiology** (2025 Volume 45 features relevant content; ongoing 2026 issues).
- **Journal of Ethnobiology and Ethnomedicine**: Regular ethnozoology papers (e.g., animal medicines).
- **Ethnobiology and Conservation**: 2025 top articles and 15th anniversary special issues (2026) on reflections and future pathways.
- Other outlets: *International Journal of Pure and Applied Zoology* perspectives and regional journals.

### Conferences and Community (2026)
- **Society of Ethnobiology 47th Annual Conference**: May 20–23, University of the South, Sewanee, Tennessee. Includes sessions on ethics, collaborations, conservation, and ethnozoology themes. 2026 Distinguished Ethnobiologist: Dr. Robert A. Bye Jr.
- **International Society of Ethnobiology (ISE) Congress**: July 26–29, Cairns, Queensland, Australia. Focus on Indigenous and local knowledge, heritage, and innovation.
- Broader wildlife events (e.g., The Wildlife Society Annual Conference, Nov 1–5, Des Moines) often include ethnozoological angles through Indigenous knowledge tracks.

The field balances urgent documentation of traditional knowledge (at risk from modernization) with actionable contributions to sustainable wildlife management, ethical de-extinction debates, and planetary health. It increasingly prioritizes community-led research, data sovereignty, and anti-colonial frameworks.
