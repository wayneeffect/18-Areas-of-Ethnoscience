**Quantum biology**, which explores quantum mechanical phenomena (such as coherence, tunneling, entanglement, and superposition) in biological systems at physiological temperatures, is maturing rapidly in 2026. The field is shifting from theoretical speculation and controversial claims toward experimental validation, quantum sensing applications, biological qubits, and interdisciplinary integration with medicine, computing, and sensing.

### Key Recent Advances (2025–2026)

- **Biological Qubits and Protein-Based Quantum Tech**: In a 2025 breakthrough published in *Nature*, University of Chicago researchers engineered cells to produce a functioning protein-based qubit (spin qubit) from a fluorescent protein. This acts as a quantum sensor for detecting subtle biological changes and opens pathways for quantum-enhanced insights into cellular processes. It was recognized as a major advance and supports the Berggren Center for Quantum Biology and Medicine.

- **Room-Temperature Quantum Effects in Biology**: Philip Kurian’s Quantum Biology Lab (and collaborators) reported experimental validations showing non-trivial quantum effects in biological materials at room temperature, measured via fluorescence quantum yield. Further confirmations were anticipated in 2025–2026. Related work includes computational studies on living systems' information processing capacity relative to the universe.

- **Quantum Sensing and Tools for Life Sciences**: Advances in nanoscale quantum biosensors, NV-center diamond magnetometry for brain mapping, hyperpolarized MRI/NMR, and quantum technologies for studying biological processes with unprecedented sensitivity. Applications include magnetoreception (e.g., animal navigation) and guiding stem cell activity with magnetic fields.

- **Quantum Computing for Biology**: Progress in using quantum algorithms for protein folding, drug design, genomics, and molecular simulation. Google launched the $10M REPLIQA program in 2026 to fund quantum computing, sensing, and AI research at five universities for biological and medical applications.

- **Photosynthesis, Enzymes, and Other Processes**: Continued exploration of quantum coherence in energy transfer, tunneling in enzymes, and potential roles in olfaction or consciousness-related structures (e.g., microtubules), with growing experimental rigor.

### Conferences and Community Momentum
- **Quantum Biology GRC 2025**: Focused on “Technology and Theory at the Intersection of Quantum Science and Biological Mechanisms” (“Quantum in Biology, Quantum for Biology”).
- **Quantum Biology Forum 2026** (April 13–14, hosted by Northwell Health / United Therapeutics): Inaugural high-level gathering on quantum effects in medicine and biology.
- **QuEBS 2026** (Budapest), Frontiers and Challenges in Quantum Biology (Copenhagen), and others.
- Special issues (e.g., *Computational and Structural Biotechnology Journal* on Advancements in Quantum Biology) and calls for papers on quantum effects in communication/information processing.

The field benefits from the 2025 International Year of Quantum Science and Technology and faces ongoing challenges around skepticism, decoherence in warm/wet environments, and the need for more reproducible experiments. It promises impacts on medicine (e.g., new diagnostics/therapies), sustainable tech (biomimetic systems), and our fundamental understanding of life.
